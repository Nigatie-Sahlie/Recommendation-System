About engineering

College of Engineering

The College of Engineering at Addis Ababa Science and Technology University (AASTU) is a premier institution dedicated to excellence in engineering education, research, and innovation. Established with the vision of becoming a center of excellence in engineering and technology, the college offers cutting-edge programs designed to prepare students for the challenges of modern engineering practice and research in Ethiopia and beyond.

Our college is committed to producing highly skilled engineers who can contribute to the technological and industrial development of the nation. With state-of-the-art laboratories, experienced faculty, and strong industry partnerships, we provide students with both theoretical knowledge and practical skills.

Academic Rules & Regulations

Important policies and guidelines for AASTU students

Admission Requirements

AASTU has specific admission requirements for all academic programs:

  • Passing score on the Ethiopian Higher Education Entrance Examination
  • Meeting specific department cut-off points
  • Original and copy of academic credentials
  • Completed application form

International students must additionally provide:

  • Equivalence certificate from Ethiopian Higher Education Relevance and Quality Agency
  • Valid student visa
  • English proficiency test results (for non-English speaking countries)
Grading System

AASTU uses the following grading scale for all courses:

  • A+: 90-100% (4.0)
  • A: 85-89% (4.0)
  • A-: 80-84% (3.75)
  • B+: 75-79% (3.5)
  • B: 70-74% (3.0)
  • B-: 65-69% (2.750)
  • C+: 60-65% (2.50)
  • C: 60-50% (2.0)
  • F: Below 50% (0.0)

Additional grading notations:

  • I: Incomplete (must be completed within 4 weeks of next semester)
  • W: Withdrawal without academic penalty
  • P: Pass (for non-graded courses)
  • NG: No grade reported
Academic Standing

Student academic status is determined by CGPA:

  • Good Standing: CGPA ≥ 2.0
  • Academic Warning: 1.5 ≤ CGPA < 2.0 for one semester
  • Academic Probation: CGPA < 1.5 for two consecutive semesters
  • Academic Dismissal: CGPA < 1.5 for three consecutive semesters

Probationary students must:

  • Meet with academic advisor to develop improvement plan
  • Limit course load to 12-15 credits
  • Repeat courses with grades of D or F
  • Achieve minimum semester GPA of 2.0 to continue
Graduation Requirements

To graduate from AASTU, students must:

  • Complete all required courses in their program with minimum CGPA of 2.0
  • Complete all practical training requirements (internship/co-op)
  • Clear all financial obligations to the university
  • Submit graduation application by published deadline
  • Complete senior project/thesis if required by department
  • Meet all attendance and conduct requirements

Additional requirements for honors:

  • Cum Laude: CGPA 3.5-3.74
  • Magna Cum Laude: CGPA 3.75-3.89
  • Summa Cum Laude: CGPA 3.9-4.0
Code of Conduct

AASTU maintains high standards of academic and personal conduct:

  • Academic Integrity: No cheating, plagiarism, or falsification of academic work
  • Attendance: Minimum 80% attendance required for all courses
  • Behavior: Respect for all members of university community
  • Property: Proper care of university facilities and equipment
  • Dress Code: Appropriate attire for academic environment

Violations may result in:

  • Warning or reprimand
  • Grade reduction or course failure
  • Suspension or dismissal
  • Legal action for serious offenses
